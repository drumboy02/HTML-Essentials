Rome

Rome is where history whispers through every street, blending ancient wonders with the vibrance of modern life. The moment that stayed with me? Standing in the Colosseum at dusk, imagining the roar of the crowds and the clash of gladiators—a vivid reminder that Rome never lets go of its past; it invites you to be part of it.

Landmarks

Colosseum
The ultimate symbol of ancient Rome, where gladiators once battled for glory. Standing within its towering arches, you can feel the echoes of history.
Pantheon
A marvel of ancient engineering with its massive dome and open oculus, inviting sunlight and rain alike into its grand interior.
Trevi Fountain
A stunning Baroque masterpiece where tossing a coin promises a return to Rome—an enduring tradition for travelers.
Roman Forum
Once the heart of Roman public life, these ruins whisper stories of emperors, orators, and the pulse of a bygone empire.

Culinary Delights

Carbonara
A Roman classic, this creamy pasta dish combines eggs, Pecorino Romano, guanciale, and black pepper—no cream needed!
Supplì
These deep-fried rice balls are stuffed with mozzarella, creating a perfect crispy exterior with a gooey, cheesy center.
Roman-style pizza
Thin, crispy, and often served al taglio (by the slice), it’s a must-try at neighborhood bakeries and pizzerias.
Cacio e pepe
Simple yet brilliant, this dish relies on Pecorino Romano and black pepper emulsified into velvety perfection.

Cultural Experiences

Evening passeggiata
Italians love a leisurely evening stroll, especially through areas like Piazza Navona or Trastevere, where street musicians and bustling cafés set the perfect mood.
Open-air opera at Caracalla Baths
Watching a live opera performance in the ruins of ancient Roman baths is a breathtaking fusion of art and history.
Exploring artisan markets
In neighborhoods like Campo de’ Fiori, local craftsmen sell handmade leather goods, jewelry, and art, offering a glimpse into Roman craftsmanship.
Aperitivo hour
Experience the social ritual of pre-dinner drinks with snacks, whether it’s a spritz with olives or a glass of wine at a rooftop bar overlooking the city.

Personal Anecdotes

The Unexpected Gelato Quest
One evening, after wandering through the charming streets of Trastevere, I stumbled upon a tiny gelateria with a long line of locals. Curious, I joined in, only to discover the most exquisite pistachio gelato I’d ever tasted—rich, nutty, and perfectly creamy. It was a simple yet unforgettable moment, proving that sometimes the best discoveries happen by accident.
Lost in the Vatican’s Grandeur
Exploring the Vatican Museums was meant to be a structured visit, but somewhere between the Raphael Rooms and the endless corridors of masterpieces, I found myself completely absorbed. Then, turning a corner, the Sistine Chapel unfolded before me—a breathtaking moment where silence filled the space and Michelangelo’s work spoke louder than words.
